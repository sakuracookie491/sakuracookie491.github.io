A familiar warmth left. The townspeople drive her out, blaming her for the death. She did not blame them for their ignorance, as old towns lack the resources to understand more, she presumes. Her fingers travel along the sky, and with a soft whisper of her blessing she casts forth a brilliant light.

Glyphs illuminate her surroundings for a moment. A star shines bright in the west; a precious reminder of the one she cherishes as Mother. Towns and towns after replay the same event. One town she finds beings similar to herself; those not human and a little different. She makes her first friend. She falls in love.

A softened heart has no barriers. With her guard down, she takes a sip from a glass that smelled a little odd. The next day she woke, no one was there. She felt amiss and sore, once pale and perfect skin littered with bruises and cuts. Tears threaten to spill, but she limps on with shallow breaths and aching legs.

The wings on her back curl. She swore the stars above whisper words of encouragement, but she could not even recall the last time she had the strength to fly, and only swears in response. Another town. She ran into bounty hunters, claiming they could exchange a Celestial's head for a large sum of money.

She grew sick of it. Sick of constantly running. Sick of being used. Sick of existence. The events unfold so quickly that vestiges of memories diminish to haphazard blurs and flashes, but it felt like moments before nothing was left of the town. She leaves, no destination in particular in mind.
